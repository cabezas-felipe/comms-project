## Executive summary

Across both sessions, monitoring remains the clear top pain and dominant workflow constraint. Session 1 identified it as the largest burden and ranked it highest (70/20/10 vs media requests and graphic production). Session 2 deepened this by showing that the core burden is not alerts coming in, but constant self-interruption to proactively check multiple sources while drafting time-critical statements.

Inbound media response is still important but appears better structured and more manageable than monitoring. The participant repeatedly frames response work as easier than carrying continuous monitoring responsibility.

The strongest build direction at this stage is to stay in interview-synthesis mode and design around reducing monitoring interruption cost, preserving speed and completeness in crisis writing.

Confidence is still 🟠 because evidence comes from one participant only, despite cross-session consistency.

## Key themes

Stack-ranked by cross-session strength and decision leverage.

### Theme 1: Monitoring is the primary pain and first-priority problem

- **Appears in:** 2 of 2 interviews
- **Longitudinal status:** Persistent
- **Segment:** Universal (for this participant's workflow)
- **Persona:** Solo bilateral comms lead
- **Confidence:** 🟠

Monitoring is both continuous and cognitively disruptive. In session 1, it is explicitly ranked as the highest-priority pain. In session 2, the mechanism is clarified: self-initiated switching during drafting to check whether the narrative has shifted.

**Supporting verbatims:**

> "The landscape monitoring... takes a lot of my space and my time... every other hour."

> [source: [Granola — baseline (16 Apr)](https://notes.granola.ai/d/bc3d8060-773b-4e87-98e6-b72076ceaa51), transcript]

> "It's just that constant interruption. Doesn't allow for smart thinking."

> [source: [Granola — baseline (16 Apr)](https://notes.granola.ai/d/bc3d8060-773b-4e87-98e6-b72076ceaa51), transcript]

> "I am interrupting myself."

> [source: [Granola — monitoring (17 Apr)](https://notes.granola.ai/d/6db72093-4248-402c-a237-be05c54e07d9), transcript]

> "No, it's terrible because that will defeat the purpose."

> [source: [Granola — monitoring (17 Apr)](https://notes.granola.ai/d/6db72093-4248-402c-a237-be05c54e07d9), transcript; context: missing updates while drafting]

### Theme 2: Monitoring behavior is mode-based (slow vs fast public story)

- **Appears in:** 1 of 2 interviews
- **Longitudinal status:** Emerging
- **Segment:** Story-mode dependent
- **Persona:** Solo bilateral comms lead
- **Confidence:** 🟠

Session 2 introduces a useful operating model: slow mode uses periodic checks (about hourly on low-intensity days), while fast public mode becomes near-continuous checking during drafting.

**Supporting verbatims:**

> "I try to monitor on a regular day. At least every hour."

> [source: [Granola — monitoring (17 Apr)](https://notes.granola.ai/d/6db72093-4248-402c-a237-be05c54e07d9), transcript]

> "Public fast... simultaneously."

> [source: [Granola — monitoring (17 Apr)](https://notes.granola.ai/d/6db72093-4248-402c-a237-be05c54e07d9), transcript]

### Theme 3: Inbound media response is high-pressure but relatively more structured

- **Appears in:** 2 of 2 interviews
- **Longitudinal status:** Persistent
- **Segment:** Universal
- **Persona:** Solo bilateral comms lead
- **Confidence:** 🟠

Response requests arrive quickly through multiple channels and require outlet-specific adaptation. Still, participant frames this as easier than removing monitoring burden.

**Supporting verbatims:**

> [source: [Granola — baseline (16 Apr)](https://notes.granola.ai/d/bc3d8060-773b-4e87-98e6-b72076ceaa51), transcript]

> "I will never respond... not knowing what they have already published."

> "Monitoring... responding to me is much... a lot easier."

> [source: [Granola — baseline (16 Apr)](https://notes.granola.ai/d/bc3d8060-773b-4e87-98e6-b72076ceaa51), transcript]

### Theme 4: Speed and completeness are both mandatory in crisis statements

- **Appears in:** 1 of 2 interviews
- **Longitudinal status:** Emerging
- **Segment:** Public high-stakes story mode
- **Persona:** Solo bilateral comms lead
- **Confidence:** 🟠

Session 2 adds a key constraint: speed cannot come at the expense of coverage, and missing key points increases narrative risk.

**Supporting verbatims:**

> "It needs to be as close to each other. It cannot be a halfway cooked message. And it needs to be fast."

> [source: [Granola — monitoring (17 Apr)](https://notes.granola.ai/d/6db72093-4248-402c-a237-be05c54e07d9), transcript]

> "What I miss keeps the story alive... my whole purpose is to kill that story."

> [source: [Granola — monitoring (17 Apr)](https://notes.granola.ai/d/6db72093-4248-402c-a237-be05c54e07d9), transcript]

## PM implications

- Build first for monitoring reduction, not generic writing assistance.
- Support explicit story-mode switching: calm cadence vs fast public cadence.
- Preserve outlet/context awareness in any response-assist features.
- Keep output laptop-first and split-view oriented.
- Treat this as directional evidence pending broader validation.
