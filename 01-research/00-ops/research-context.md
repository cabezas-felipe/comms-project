## Personas / segments

**A (behavioral):** **Solo bilateral comms lead** — one person handling landscape monitoring, drafting (statements, press-adjacent copy), and coordination across two languages and markets (e.g. Colombia–US, embassy-style public comms).

**Goals / jobs (refined after P1 session 2):**

- **Early narrative signal** — know what moved since last check; avoid gaps that keep a story alive.
- **Mode-appropriate cadence** — *calm / non-public slow:* periodic checks (~hourly baseline on lighter days); *fast public:* near-continuous self-checking while drafting.
- **Credible timed responses** — inbound media with outlet-specific framing; read published copy before responding.
- **Crisis-grade statements** — speed and completeness both non-negotiable in high-stakes reactive writing; monitoring is an **information** input to drafting, not a substitute for judgment.
- **Crisis loop** — detection → leadership context → fact-check / clarifications → bilingual drafts → distribution (with possible **shared monitoring** in crisis so one person can focus on writing).

**Pains (P1, two sessions — still directional):**

1. **Interruptive monitoring** — especially self-initiated switching (split screen: drafting + browser/Twitter/Google keyword scan); fear of missing updates while a statement is still open.
2. **Multi-channel inbound media** — urgent, deadline-compressed; ranked second in explicit 70/20/10 split vs monitoring.
3. **Graphic / promotional production** — real but third in improvement budget.

**Open (persona-level):** safe down-ranking rules; alert trust; minimum signal to **stop** monitoring and polish; embassy IT/legal/vendor constraints; replication beyond this one operator.

## Beliefs

**Confirmed (evidence so far — still one participant, two sessions; treat as strong directional for P1 only):**

- **Monitoring is the #1 improvement target** vs inbound media and graphic work: explicit **70 / 20 / 10** split and “monitoring first” when forced to choose (session 1).
- **Core pain is interruptive scanning while writing**, not only inbound pings: self-interruption, split-screen workflow, keyword-driven checks while a draft is open (session 2).
- **Calm vs fast public modes** change cadence: ~hourly checks possible on slower days vs near-continuous checking during a fast public story (session 2).
- **Speed and completeness are both mandatory** for crisis-style statements; missing angles can extend the story (session 2).
- **Laptop / large screen strongly preferred** for this joint task; phone is a poor fit (no practical split view, writing friction, notification noise) (session 2).

**Assumed (needs validation):**

- “Solo” is default day-to-day; **crisis mode** may **share monitoring** across colleagues so one person can concentrate on producing copy (session 1).
- Tacit triage rules (reputation, novelty, traction, legal/mission sensitivity) can be partially externalized into product **if** trust and false-positive/negative tradeoffs are tested with real cases.

**Unknown / not yet evidenced:**

- Prevalence of P1’s ranking and modes across other solo bilateral operators, institutions, or private-sector comms.
- Quantified time on task; full channel/tool inventory beyond what P1 named.
- Whether a digest / “what changed” / ranked feed actually reduces interruption **without** increasing misses (requires evaluative study).
- Regulatory and procurement reality for any shipped product.
