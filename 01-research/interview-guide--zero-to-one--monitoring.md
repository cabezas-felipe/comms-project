# Interview guide | Zero to one | Monitoring

**Decision:** Which monitoring problem to solve first to improve writing speed and quality under pressure.

**Hypothesis:** The core pain is interruption + re-entry cost while writing, not lack of information.

**Question type:** Generative (problem discovery only; no solution pitching).

**Session length:** 60 minutes

**How to run:** Keep it conversational. Ask one question at a time. Stay in real episodes (not hypotheticals) until late validation. Separate observation from interpretation.

---

## 0:00-0:05 — Framing and consent

Use your own voice:

- "Thanks for doing this. I want to focus on monitoring and how it affects your writing workflow in real time."
- "This is not a performance review; it is product discovery."
- "Please avoid confidential names or sensitive details. We can keep examples generic."
- "I am not testing features today. I only want to understand your workflow, decision rules, and pressure points."

**Interviewer check before moving on:**

- Confirm one recent writing-heavy day she can replay in detail.

---

## 0:05-0:20 — Concrete episode replay (minute-by-minute)

Goal: Reconstruct one recent episode where writing and monitoring happened at the same time.

1. "Pick one recent case where you were drafting and also monitoring at the same time. Start from when the task began."
2. "What were you writing, and what made it high-stakes (or not)?"
3. "Walk me minute-by-minute through what you did first, second, third."
4. "At each interruption, what signal appeared, where did it appear, and what did you do next?"
5. "What changed in your draft because of monitoring input, and what stayed unchanged?"
6. "Where did you lose momentum or need to re-enter context?"

**Probes (use only when needed):**

- "What were you looking at right before the interruption?"
- "How long did it take to regain your place in the draft?"
- "Did the interruption improve the final response, or just delay it?"
- "Which interruption felt necessary vs avoidable?"

**Disconfirming probe:**

- "In that same episode, was there a moment where active monitoring clearly made your writing better, faster, or more accurate?"

---

## 0:20-0:35 — Mode mapping: public/fast vs non-public/slower

Goal: Validate mode transitions and how pressure changes monitoring behavior.

1. "Compare two cases: one public fast-evolving story and one non-public/slower request."
2. "How do you decide which mode you are in?"
3. "What changes between modes in how often you check for updates?"
4. "What changes in your risk tolerance for missing an update?"
5. "What changes in who else is involved, if anyone?"
6. "What is the earliest sign that you need to switch from calmer mode to crisis-like mode?"

**Probes:**

- "What is non-negotiable to monitor in both modes?"
- "What can wait in calm mode but not in public mode?"
- "When does speed beat completeness, and when does completeness beat speed?"

**Disconfirming probe:**

- "Have you had public stories where lower monitoring cadence still worked fine? What made that possible?"

---

## 0:35-0:50 — Signal triage and trust rules

Goal: Understand what makes a signal actionable enough to interrupt writing.

1. "When a new mention appears, how do you decide if it deserves immediate attention?"
2. "What source characteristics increase trust? What lowers trust?"
3. "How do you treat chatter from lower-tier outlets or low-credibility social posts?"
4. "What confirmation steps do you do before adapting messaging?"
5. "What is your rule for acting on one signal vs waiting for corroboration?"
6. "Describe a false alarm and what you learned from it."

**Probes:**

- "Do language, geography, or outlet type change your threshold?"
- "What is the minimum evidence needed to interrupt drafting?"
- "What is the cost of overreacting vs underreacting in your context?"

**Disconfirming probe:**

- "Can you recall a time when ignoring an early signal was the right call?"

---

## 0:50-0:57 — Environment and switching-cost map

Goal: Map tools/surfaces and estimate context-switch burden.

1. "What devices and apps are open while you are writing and monitoring?"
2. "Where do you draft by default, and when do you switch surfaces?"
3. "How often do you move between laptop, phone, messaging apps, and browser feeds?"
4. "Which switch is most expensive cognitively?"
5. "Do you start responses from scratch or from prior structures/templates?"

**Probes:**

- "Which transitions are smooth, and which cause rework?"
- "What kind of writing task is hardest to resume after interruption?"

---

## 0:57-1:00 — Wrap and model validation

1. "I’m hearing that monitoring is a core dependency of writing, and pressure changes by story mode. What did I miss?"
2. "If we only clarified one thing better next time, what should it be?"
3. "Is there any monitoring rule you use that we did not capture?"

## Immediate post-call synthesis (10 min)

1. What percentage of interruptions were necessary in hindsight?
2. Which mode transition caused the biggest performance drop?
3. What cadence threshold seemed acceptable per mode?
4. Which trust rule most strongly governs interruption decisions?
5. What single unresolved ambiguity should guide Interview #3 or next validation step?
