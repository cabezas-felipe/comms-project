# Interview guide | Zero to one

**Goal:** Identify and rank top comms pain points/opportunities, then pick one first prototype priority.

**How to run:** Keep it conversational. Ask one question at a time. Let silence work. Ask for concrete examples.

## 0:00-0:05 — Intro

Use your own voice:

- "Thanks for doing this. I want to understand your week-to-week comms workflow so we can choose one thing to improve first."
- "This is not a performance review; it is product discovery for our side project."
- "I’ll take notes. If we ever record or screenshot, I’ll ask first."
- "Please skip anything sensitive or work-confidential."

## 0:05-0:12 — Top 3 issues (diverge)

1. "In a normal week, what are the top three things in comms that take the most time, create the most stress, or distract you from your core work?"
2. "Let’s list all three before we go deeper."

Capture the three items in plain language.

## 0:12-0:25 — Concrete examples (ground)

For each of the three:

1. "Can you walk me through one recent real example from start to finish?"
2. "Where did it slow down, break, or get reworked?"
3. "Who was involved at each step?"

## 0:25-0:35 — Frequency, impact, and coping (probe)

For each issue:

1. "How often does this happen?"
2. "What happens when it goes badly?"
3. "What workarounds or coping habits do you use, and which feel sustainable over time?"

## 0:35-0:42 — Forced priority (converge)

1. "If we could improve only one of these first, which one should we pick and why?"
2. Optional tie-breaker: "If you had 100 points, how would you split them across the top three?"

## 0:42-0:45 — Close

1. "Quick check: does this change during a bad week vs a normal week?"
2. "Did we miss any issue that should be in the top three?"
3. "Based on this, I’ll draft a proposed #1 target and we can sanity-check it."

## What to write down during the call

- Top 3 issues (exact wording)
- One concrete example per issue
- Frequency estimate
- Impact when it fails
- Current workaround/coping behavior
- Chosen #1 priority + why
